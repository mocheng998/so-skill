# Etsy Search Algorithm Guide

## Core Algorithm Principles

Etsy's search algorithm aims to provide buyers with the most relevant and highest quality search results. The algorithm primarily considers the following factors:

### 1. Relevance

**Exact Matching**
- When the keywords a buyer searches for exactly match the keywords in the listing title or tags, that listing receives a higher relevance score
- The order and completeness of keywords matter
- Example: When a buyer searches for "chunky knit blanket", listings with the complete phrase "chunky knit blanket" in the title rank higher than those with only scattered words like "chunky", "knit", "blanket"

**Broad Matching**
- Even when keywords don't match exactly, the algorithm considers related words and concepts
- Including individual components of the search terms in the title still adds value
- Example: When a buyer searches for "chunky knit blanket", a listing titled "Knit Blanket - Chunky Wool Throw" remains relevant

**Keyword Position Weighting**
- Keywords at the front of the title carry more weight than those at the end
- Keywords in tags also participate in matching, but with lower weight than the title
- Keywords in the listing description carry the lowest weight

### 2. Quality Score

Quality Score is the algorithm's comprehensive evaluation of a listing's overall performance, including:

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**
- How frequently a listing is clicked when it appears in search results
- High CTR indicates the listing is appealing to buyers

**Conversion Rate (Conversion Rate)**
- The proportion of clicks that result in actual purchases
- High conversion rate indicates the listing meets buyer needs

**Favorites & Cart Adds**
- When buyers add listings to their favorites or shopping cart
- Indicates purchase intent

**Listing Recency**
- Newly published or recently updated listings may receive a short-term boost
- Long-term performance still depends on other quality metrics

**Shop Performance**
- The shop's overall ratings, shipping speed, and customer service quality
- Listings from high-quality shops may receive additional weighting

### 3. Timeline for Building Quality Score

**60-90 Day Observation Period**
- Newly published listings need 60-90 days for the algorithm to collect sufficient data
- During this period, the algorithm tests how listings perform across different search queries
- Avoid frequently modifying titles and tags during this period, as it may interfere with data collection

**Data Accumulation Process**
- The algorithm records impressions, clicks, and conversions for each listing
- Based on this data, it calculates the listing's relevance and quality score
- Once scores stabilize, listings receive consistent rankings for their corresponding search queries

## Optimization Strategies

### Title Optimization Principles

1. **Place core keywords first**: Put the most important "superstar keywords" at the front of the title
2. **Prioritize complete phrases**: Use complete keyword phrases when possible to earn exact matching weighting
3. **Keep it natural and readable**: Titles should make sense to human buyers; avoid keyword stuffing
4. **Use all available characters**: Etsy titles allow up to 140 characters—use the full space

### Tag Optimization Principles

1. **Repeat title keywords**: Include core keywords from the title in your tags as well
2. **Add long-tail keywords**: Use tags to supplement relevant long-tail terms that couldn't fit in the title
3. **Use all 13 tags**: Etsy allows up to 13 tags per listing—use them all
4. **Multi-word tags**: Each tag can contain multiple words (e.g., "chunky knit blanket"); don't split them up

### Continuous Optimization Strategies

1. **Monitor performance regularly**: Check keyword search volume, competition level, and ranking changes monthly
2. **A/B testing**: Change only one variable at a time (e.g., title or tags) and observe the results
3. **Seasonal adjustments**: Adjust keyword strategy based on season and trends
4. **Learn from competitors**: Analyze the title and tag strategies of top-ranking competitors
