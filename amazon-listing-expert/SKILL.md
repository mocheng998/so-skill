## TD Template (Title & Description — Bullet Points)

- **Quantity**: Maximum 7 bullet points.
- **Format**: Selling Point — Description.
- **Content**: Follow the "4+2" formula:
  - **4 selling-point bullets**: Each bullet combines a selling point with a description that naturally incorporates consumer search keywords. Example: `Optimized 900-watt motor and refined nutrient extraction blades blend the toughest whole foods into nutritious shakes, smoothies, and nut butters.`
  - **2 supplementary bullets**: 1 bullet listing "What's in the Box" contents, and 1 bullet with warranty/care reminders or notes.
  - **1 optional bullet** (7th): Additional selling point or compatibility info if needed.

**Key Principles:**
- Focus on benefits, not just features
- Weave consumer search keywords naturally into descriptions — avoid keyword stuffing
- Keep each bullet concise but informative
- Each bullet follows the pattern: **Selling Point** — Description

**Example** (for a blender):
```
• Powerful 900W Motor — Optimized 900-watt motor and refined nutrient extraction blades blend the toughest whole foods into nutritious shakes, smoothies, and nut butters
• Easy One-Touch Operation — Simple single-serve design with one-touch blending and pulse function for consistent results every time
• BPA-Free Materials — All cups and lids are made from durable, BPA-free Tritan plastic, safe for daily use
• Compact Space-Saving Design — Fits easily on any countertop, perfect for small kitchens, dorms, and offices
• What's in the Box — 1x motor base, 1x 24oz cup, 1x 18oz cup, 2x flip-top lids, 1x lip ring, recipe book
• 1-Year Warranty — Full manufacturer warranty with dedicated customer support; contact us for any questions
```

## Image Template: X + 1

The image template uses a combination of "X vertical images + 1 video" to cater to mobile-first browsing habits.

**Vertical Image Content & Sequence:**

| Slot | Type | Content | Guidelines |
|------|------|---------|------------|
| 1 | **Main product image** (light gray/white background) | Product centered, clean composition | Keep it clean; 20-30% padding on all sides; show full product |
| 2-5 | **Selling point lifestyle images** (3-4 images) | Product in real-use scenarios with selling point text overlay | Scene must match the selling point; include brief text callout |
| 6 | **Accessories / bundle image** | Flat-lay of all included items | Show everything in the box clearly; include receipt/manual if applicable |
| 7 | **Size / dimension reference** | Product next to familiar objects for scale | Help buyers understand actual size |

**Video Content Guidelines:**
- Match the style and tone of the lifestyle images
- Focus on demonstrating the complete use/assembly process
- Weave selling points as text overlays during the demonstration

**Image Requirements:**
- Minimum 2000x2000 pixels (supports zoom)
- Vertical orientation preferred (mobile-friendly)
- Consistent visual style across all images
- High-quality lighting, accurate colors

## A+ Product Description (EBC) — "1+8+1+8+1" Structure

The Product Description page (A+ / Enhanced Brand Content) uses a "1+8+1+8+1" structure, appearing below the standard listing after Amazon's built-in recommendation widgets.

| Section | Content | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **1st "1"** | Product hero slogan | Full-width image or video with main value proposition |
| **1st "8"** | Feature selling points (8 modules) | Data-driven feature descriptions; iterate based on performance analytics |
| **2nd "1"** | Lifestyle hero slogan | Full-width lifestyle image or video |
| **2nd "8"** | Scenario selling points (8 modules) | Use-case and lifestyle descriptions; primarily informed by review analysis |
| **3rd "1"** | Brand story | Customer count (e.g., crowdfunding backers), countries served, team intro, brand history, certifications |

**Content Guidelines:**
- 1st set of 8 modules: Focus on product features and technical advantages; data-driven, iterate based on analytics
- 2nd set of 8 modules: Focus on usage scenarios and customer pain points; primarily informed by review analysis
- Brand story section: Build trust through social proof (customer numbers, awards, certifications)
- Each module: image + text pair; keep text concise and scannable
- Use competitor analysis to identify gaps and differentiation opportunities

## Workflow

### Standard Listing Creation Process

1. **Research Phase**
   - Analyze 3-5 competitor listings
   - Identify keyword gaps and content differentiation opportunities
   - Review customer feedback to understand pain points and selling angles

2. **TD Creation Phase**
   - Identify top 4 selling points from research
   - Write 7 bullet points following the "4+2" formula
   - Ensure consumer search keywords are woven naturally into descriptions

3. **Image Planning Phase**
   - Plan the "X+1" image set based on selling points from TD
   - Write detailed image briefs for each slot
   - Ensure scenes match their corresponding selling points

4. **A+ Content Phase**
   - Build the "1+8+1+8+1" page structure
   - Feature modules based on product data; scenario modules based on review analysis
   - Include brand story with trust-building elements

5. **Review and Optimize Phase**
   - Cross-check all components for consistency (selling points should align across TD, images, and A+)
   - Verify keyword coverage across bullet points and A+ content
   - Ensure no duplication — A+ content should complement, not repeat, bullet points verbatim

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Writing more than 7 bullet points | The "4+2" structure ensures complete coverage without overwhelming readers |
| Feature-only descriptions | Listing specs without benefits; always lead with what the customer gains |
| Low-resolution or inconsistent images | Images below 2000x2000 can't zoom; inconsistent styles look unprofessional |
| Duplicating bullet points in A+ | A+ should expand and complement, not repeat verbatim |
| Ignoring competitor analysis | Missed differentiation opportunities; always research first |
| Keyword stuffing | Reduces readability and hurts conversion; keywords should flow naturally |
| Mismatched images and selling points | Each lifestyle image must correspond to a specific selling point |
