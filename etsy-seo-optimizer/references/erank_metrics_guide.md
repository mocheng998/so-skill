# eRank Key Metrics Guide

## Core Metrics Overview

### 1. Search Volume

**Definition**
- Estimated number of times a specific keyword is searched on Etsy per month
- Reflects the level of buyer demand for that keyword

**Interpretation**
- **High Search Volume (>10,000/month)**: Indicates strong buyer demand, but competition is typically intense
- **Medium Search Volume (1,000-10,000/month)**: Balanced opportunity with reasonable demand and relatively manageable competition
- **Low Search Volume (<1,000/month)**: Niche market with little competition but limited demand

**Usage Tips**
- New shops or new listings: Prioritize low to medium search volume keywords ("Low-Hanging Fruit")
- Established shops: May target high search volume keywords ("High-Hanging Fruit")
- Combination strategy: Include 1-2 high search volume keywords in the title + multiple medium/low search volume long-tail keywords

### 2. Competition

**Definition**
- Number of listings using that keyword
- Reflects the market saturation level of the keyword

**Interpretation**
- **High Competition (>50,000 listings)**: Market saturated, new listings struggle to gain exposure
- **Medium Competition (10,000-50,000 listings)**: Competitive but opportunities still exist
- **Low Competition (<10,000 listings)**: Easier to achieve rankings, but verify sufficient search volume

**Usage Tips**
- Calculate "search volume/competition" ratio; higher ratio indicates greater value
- Avoid keywords with "high competition + low search volume"
- Prioritize keywords with "low competition + medium search volume"

### 3. Click-Through Rate (CTR)

**Definition**
- Proportion of search results where listings are clicked by buyers
- Reflects the appeal of listings on the search results page

**Interpretation**
- **High CTR (>5%)**: Listing images, title, and price appeal to buyers
- **Medium CTR (2-5%)**: Normal level
- **Low CTR (<2%)**: May need to optimize main image, title, or price

**Usage Tips**
- Low CTR may not indicate a keyword issue; it could be a listing presentation problem
- Improving main image quality, title appeal, and price competitiveness can boost CTR
- Monitor CTR changes to evaluate optimization effectiveness

### 4. Engagement Rate

**Definition**
- Degree of buyer interaction with listings (clicks, favorites, add to cart, purchases)
- Comprehensively reflects overall appeal and conversion capability of listings

**Interpretation**
- **High Engagement Rate**: Listing meets buyer needs, high quality score
- **Low Engagement Rate**: May need to optimize listing description, images, price, or keyword relevance

**Usage Tips**
- Engagement rate is an important component of quality score
- Continuously optimize listing pages to improve engagement rate
- Keywords with low engagement may not suit the listing; consider replacing them

## Keyword Strategy Framework

### "Low-Hanging Fruit" Strategy

**Characteristics**
- Low to medium competition
- Medium search volume
- Higher search volume/competition ratio

**Best For**
- New shops or new listings
- Gaining initial traffic and sales quickly
- Building the foundation for quality score

**Example**
- Search Volume: 2,000/month
- Competition: 5,000 listings
- Ratio: 0.4 (relatively high)

### "High-Hanging Fruit" Strategy

**Characteristics**
- High competition
- High search volume
- Lower search volume/competition ratio

**Best For**
- Established shops and high quality score listings
- Pursuing greater traffic and exposure
- Long-term optimization goals

**Example**
- Search Volume: 50,000/month
- Competition: 100,000 listings
- Ratio: 0.5 (though competition is fierce, search volume is substantial)

### Combination Strategy

**Title Construction**
1. **Primary Keywords (1-2)**: "Superstar keywords" with high search volume
2. **Secondary Keywords (2-3)**: Related keywords with medium search volume
3. **Long-tail Keywords (1-2)**: Specific descriptive words with low competition

**Tag Construction**
1. Repeat core keywords from the title
2. Add long-tail keywords that cannot fit in the title
3. Include relevant category and attribute words (e.g., "gift", "handmade", "custom")

## eRank Workflow

1. **Enter Core Keywords**: Start with broad product category words (e.g., "blanket")
2. **Review Suggested Keywords**: eRank displays related keywords and their metrics
3. **Filter Keywords**:
   - Sort by search volume to find high-demand keywords
   - Sort by competition to find low-competition keywords
   - Calculate ratios to find the best balance
4. **Build Keyword List**:
   - Select 1-2 "superstar keywords" (high search volume)
   - Select 3-5 "Low-Hanging Fruit" keywords (medium search volume + low competition)
   - Select 5-8 long-tail keywords (low search volume + low competition)
5. **Organize into Title and Tags**:
   - Title: Superstar keywords + secondary keywords + long-tail keywords
   - Tags: Repeat title keywords + additional long-tail keywords
